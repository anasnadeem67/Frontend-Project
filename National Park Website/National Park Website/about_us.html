About Us



The “National Park” is largest Pakistani Park. The Magical landscape of National Park is well known and fabled for its tiger richness. Established in the year 1985 as National Park, has the glory of being Pakistan's oldest and most prestigious National Park. pans over an extent of 520 square kilometers, its whole area comprises of hills, marshy depressions, riverine belts, grasslands and large lake. Nature watch and wildlife viewing in the park is done in an open four wheeler Jeep and on elephant back. Sheltering a healthy population of tigers and rare species like Otters and the endemic fish eating crocodile, the national park is one of the most sought after destinations for the wildlife buffs.

National Park, the oldest tiger reserve forest in Pakistan, is a highly popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts. Thousands of people from all over the world used to visit here to have a tryst with one of the most ruthless predators of the wild forest, the Bengal Tiger. Being a reserved natural habitat for plenty of wild animals, all the tourism activity in the forest is managed by the forest department of the govt. of Punjab state.

If you love bird watching then National Park is virtual haven for such tourists. National Park and its adjoining area is a home to more than 650 species of residents and migratory birds. Particularly Dhikala is fine place to look for birds of prey, more than over 50 species of raptors alone shows the healthy biodiversity of the area. Their multiplex behavior is intriguing and their varied songs are very much pleasing to the ear. In a nutshell, this finest national park of Pakistan is well known for rich and varied wildlife including royal Bengal tiger, elephant, four to five species of deer and rich birdlife.

The National Park, an oldest national park of Pakistan, is the prime wildlife destination for the wildlife loving as well as nature loving tourists from all over the world. The park is located in the Islamabad, a city of Punjab state of Pakistan. Being in the proximity of the National Capital City of Pakistan, the park is easily accessible through the Railway and Roadway routes.

Entry Process:

In order to make an entry inside the reserve park, you need to obtain the entry permit from the concern authority. Without the entry permit, you will not be allowed inside the park in any condition. There are two types of tourism services provided by the forest department at the National Park. One is Day Safari and other is an Accommodation at FRH. The day safari permit can be obtained at the entry gate of the respective zone while the permit for night halt at different forest rest houses located inside the forest is issued at the reception office of the Corbett reserve park in the Ramnagar city. Also, the day safari or accommodation at FRH can also be booked online in advance. Once you obtain the permit, neither it is transferable nor refundable.

Jungle Safari in National Park:

National Park offers enthralling wildlife safari amidst its jungle with the options like Jeep Safari, Elephant Safari and Canter Safari for the wildlife enthusiast. Tourists may have safaris through the jungle of National Park and if lucky enough can even catch Royal Bengal Tigers.

Jeep Sari in National Park:

Jeep Safari is the most feasible option to discover the true wilderness and varied species of flora and fauna found in the National Park. Jeep is a four wheeler vehicle permitted to enter through all buffer zones of the park. It is the most popular way because Jeep is smaller in size when compared with canter that takes you easily some of the unexplored and hidden corners of the jungle where we could easily spot tigers. Jeep Safari can be done in tourism zones like Bijrani, Jhirna, Durgadevi, Dhela and Sitabani.

Food and Canteen:

ourists to the National Park might be worried what to eat while staying inside the forest lodges. But we need not to take concern of these facilities as forest department has set up food canteens inside the Bijrani, Gairal and Dhikala Forest lodges. Guest coming to the park is served with basic Pakistan cuisines. Please note that consumption of non-vegetarian food and alcohol is strictly prohibited inside the rest houses.

Forest Lodges:

For better tourism management and to make equal distribution of the flow of visitors the National Park has been divided in to five tourism zone and each zone offers night stay inside the forest rest houses that is erected by the forest officials. Tourists are allowed to make reservation minimum for 01 Night and maximum for 03 nights only. Government recognized forest houses are Bijrani Forest Rest House, Malani (inside Bijrani Tourism Zone), Sultan, Gairal, Sarapduli, khinnanauli and Dhikla forest house (inside Dhikala Zone) and Jhirna inside the Jhirna tourism zone.

Some More Facilities & Services :

In order maximize the wildlife sightings and complete tour around National Park, National Park provides trained nature guides that accompany you while roaming inside the jungle. These naturalist or guide is registered with the forest officials and help tourists in spotting the tigers and not to lose your way inside the forest. National Park is paradise for such tourists who love elephant ride amidst jungle. An elephant rides accompanied by trained Mahout is offered at Bijrani, Dhikala, Khinnanauli, Gairal and Jhirna. You have to obtain permit for elephant ride that is conducted in two shifts - morning and evening in each of the respective zones.
